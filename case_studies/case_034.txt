=== CASE STUDY PROFILE ===
Topic: Anticipatory Grief and Complicated Bereavement

=== CLINICAL METADATA ===
Caregiver Profile: Adult child of a deceased parent, likely in their 20s or 30s
Clinical Goal: Help the client process and work through their unresolved grief, develop coping strategies to manage anxiety and depression, and encourage self-compassion and seeking support.

=== PATIENT EMOTIONAL PROFILE ===
Primary Emotions: Sadness, Fear, Guilt
Cognitive State: Catastrophizing, Avoidance
Underlying Need: Emotional safety and control

=== COMMUNICATION TECHNIQUES USED ===
Active listening (acknowledging the client's pain), Validation (acknowledging the normalcy of their experience), Reframing (encouraging a new perspective on coping with emotions).

=== THE TRANSCRIPT ===
User: I've been struggling with feelings of anxiety and depression for years now. It all started when my father passed away suddenly when I was just 15. I've tried to cope by avoiding difficult emotions and situations, but it feels like I'm drowning in a sea of sadness and fear.
Counselor: I'm deeply sorry for the loss you've experienced and the pain it's caused you...
