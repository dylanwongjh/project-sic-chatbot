=== CASE STUDY PROFILE ===
Topic: Anticipatory grief and emotional avoidance in terminal illness

=== CLINICAL METADATA ===
Caregiver Profile: Adult child of terminally ill parent (inferred)
Clinical Goal: To help the client process their emotions, acknowledge their feelings, and develop effective coping strategies for anticipatory grief.

=== PATIENT EMOTIONAL PROFILE ===
Primary Emotions: Overwhelmed, Sadness, Guilt
Cognitive State: Catastrophizing, Emotional avoidance
Underlying Need: Emotional safety and control

=== COMMUNICATION TECHNIQUES USED ===
Active listening (acknowledging the client's experience), Validation (recognizing the complexity of grief and its impact on emotions), Reframing (explaining emotional avoidance as a coping mechanism), Empowerment (suggesting expressive activities, support groups, and mental health professionals to promote self-regulation).
