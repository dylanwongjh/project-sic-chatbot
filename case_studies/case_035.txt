=== CASE STUDY PROFILE ===
Topic: Anticipatory Grief and Complex Bereavement

=== CLINICAL METADATA ===
Caregiver Profile: Adult child (likely in their 20s or 30s) who experienced sudden loss of a parent at a young age.
Clinical Goal: To help the client process and work through their grief, rather than avoiding it, and to encourage seeking support.

=== PATIENT EMOTIONAL PROFILE ===
Primary Emotions: Anxiety, Depression, Sadness
Cognitive State: Catastrophizing (avoiding difficult emotions and situations)
Underlying Need: Emotional safety and control

=== COMMUNICATION TECHNIQUES USED ===
Active listening, Validation, Reframing (helping the client reframe their avoidance of emotions as a part of the healing process), Empathy.

=== THE TRANSCRIPT ===
User: I've been struggling with feelings of anxiety and depression for years now. It all started when my father passed away suddenly when I was just 15. I've tried to cope by avoiding difficult emotions and situations, but it feels like I'm drowning in a sea of sadness and fear.
Counselor: I'm deeply sorry for the loss you've experienced and the pain it's caused you...
