Topic: Anticipatory grief and caregiver guilt

=== CLINICAL METADATA ===
Caregiver Profile: Adult child (age not specified) grieving the loss of their mother
Clinical Goal: To help the client process and manage their intense feelings of guilt, explore coping mechanisms, and develop a healthier perspective on their relationship with their mother.
Summary: A daughter is consumed by overwhelming guilt and grief after her mother's passing, feeling responsible for not doing enough to save her, and seeks help to find closure and process her emotions.

Primary Emotions: Guilt, Sadness, Helplessness
Cognitive State: Catastrophizing, Rumination
Underlying Need: Emotional safety and control

=== COMMUNICATION TECHNIQUES USED ===
- Empathy (acknowledging the client's pain)
- Validation (recognizing the complexity of grief)
- Reframing (suggesting a shift from guilt to processing emotions)
- Future-oriented planning (setting goals for future sessions)

User: I've been feeling overwhelmed by the guilt I've been carrying since my mother passed away. I can't seem to shake off the feeling that I didn't do enough for her, and I've been spending hours researching various treatments and protocols, hoping to find something that could have saved her.
