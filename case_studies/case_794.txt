=== CASE STUDY PROFILE ===
Topic: Grief and Problem-Solving Difficulties

=== CLINICAL METADATA ===
Caregiver Profile: Adult child (age not specified)
Clinical Goal: To help the client identify and articulate their problem-solving process, improve decision-making, and conflict resolution skills in the context of grief.
Summary: A grieving adult child is struggling to make decisions and resolve conflicts due to feelings of overwhelm after losing her mother, seeking a better understanding of her problem-solving process.

=== PATIENT EMOTIONAL PROFILE ===
Primary Emotions: Sadness, Overwhelm, Loss
Cognitive State: Catastrophizing, Flooding (implied by difficulty in making decisions)
Underlying Need: Emotional safety and control

=== COMMUNICATION TECHNIQUES USED ===
- Empathy (acknowledging the client's loss)
- Normalization (explaining that grief can affect problem-solving abilities)
- Problem-focused coping (introducing a specific intervention to address the issue)

=== THE TRANSCRIPT ===
User: I've been struggling to understand my problem-solving process, and the steps I naturally go through. The recent passing of my mother has left me feeling lost and overwhelmed, and I've found myself unable to make decisions or resolve conflicts effectively.
Counselor: I'm truly sorry for your loss. It's not uncommon for individuals dealing with grief to experience difficulty in their problem-solving abilities. The problem-solving intervention we discussed can be a valuable tool in helping you navigate this challenging time. By articulating your thought process out loud, you'll be able to identify the specific steps you take when dealing with a problem, even if they occur quickly or over an extended period.
